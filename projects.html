Tiger Nut Drying Machine

July 2012

Engineered an automated drying system specifically designed for tiger nut processing. This machine optimized the drying process while preserving nutritional content for use in poultry feed formulation.

Technical Specifications:

  • Automated temperature control system
  • Uniform heat distribution mechanism
  • Reduced drying time by 60% compared to traditional methods
  • Capacity-optimized for small to medium-scale operations

Achievements:

Successfully reduced tiger nut drying time while maintaining nutritional value, making it a viable alternative to maize in poultry feed. Conducted comprehensive nutrient analysis to validate results.

Bio-oils and Biodiesel Extraction Research

August 2020

Conducted comprehensive research on extracting bio-oils and biodiesel from African oil bean seeds, investigating the effects of thermal pre-treatments on bio-oil quality and yield.

Research Focus:

  • Feedstock analysis and optimization
  • Thermal pre-treatment methodology
  • Bio-oil quality assessment and characterization
  • Sustainable energy technology applications

Outcomes:

Contributed valuable insights to sustainable energy technology development, demonstrating the viability of African oil bean seeds as a feedstock for bio-oil and biodiesel production. Research findings have implications for renewable energy initiatives.
